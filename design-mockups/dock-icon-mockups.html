stavR dock icon — concepts

Six directions for the Governor app icon — all built on the Raido rune, none using a circle. Each is shown large on a light and a dark surface, plus a small dock-size render, so you can see how it holds up tiny.

1 · Iron squircle
The modern OS-standard tile — the literal shape of every current macOS and Windows app icon. Restrained, premium, and it answers "not a circle" directly.
2 · Rust squircle
Same modern tile, but rust is the fill — the brand colour carries the whole icon. Warmer, more vivid, harder to miss in a crowded dock.
3 · Negative cutout
A rust tile with the rune knocked out — defined by absence. Distinctive and deliberate; the rune shifts with whatever surface sits behind it.
4 · Runestone slab
A tall iron tablet — the most literal nod to the runestone heritage. Strong brand story, but a slab shape sits a little oddly among square dock neighbours.
5 · Hexagon
The rune in a hex tile — a callback to the topology dashboard's node language. Geometric and techy; ties the app icon to the product's own visual world.
6 · Bare glyph
Just the rune, no tile. Minimal and iconic in isolation — but it floats in a dock and is a weaker click target next to solid neighbours.

My read — a squircle tile is the correct modern default: it is the literal shape of every current macOS and Windows app icon, and it fixes the circle directly (concepts 1 and 2). For something more distinctive that is still a proper, clickable tile, the negative cutout (3) is the strongest — the rune defined by absence reads as deliberate and premium. The runestone slab (4) carries the most brand story but sits awkwardly among square dock neighbours; the hexagon (5) ties the icon to the dashboard's node language; and I would steer away from the bare glyph (6) for a dock icon — elegant alone, but it floats. My pick: 3 for distinctiveness, or 1 if you want restrained. Whichever you choose becomes the basis for the full icon set at every resolution macOS and Windows need. Colours here are placeholders from the iron palette — they get tuned in the real asset.
