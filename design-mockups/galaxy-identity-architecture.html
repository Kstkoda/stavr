Architecture drawing for proposed/galaxy-identity-trust-design.md — identity, trust, the hub mesh, and communication, across the five modes.

1 · The spine — identity is a key you hold

Every device and node has its own keypair. The Seal — your one irreplaceable secret — only signs a certificate for each. Lose a laptop and you revoke one certificate; the Seal is untouched. No identity provider, nothing outside the galaxy.

2 · Identity is not trust — two separate planes

A cryptographically verified key proves only that a message is real. Whether to trust the holder is a different question, answered by a separate, graded layer. The hub confers nothing — being seen on a hub is not trust.

3 · Joining a hub — four ways to earn trust

① STRONGEST
Invite capability
A one-time, scoped, expiring invite signed by someone with authority. Carries a bootstrap hub address too.
②
Vouch — or K-of-N
Trusted members sign introductions. A hub can require K independent vouches; vouchers are accountable.
③
Verifiable credential
A credential from an issuer the hub's members already trust. Checked offline.
④ FALLBACK
TOFU + confirm
Accept on first contact, then confirm a short safety-number out of band. The SSH model.
In every case the hub itself decides nothing — it only carried the introduction. Threshold vouching (K-of-N) is the strong option: independent vouchers, each named in the transparency log, each with their own trust score at stake.

4 · The hub mesh — one node type, hubs that emerge

Every node ships identical — daemon and hub-candidate. A node becomes a load-bearing hub by being chosen, not elected: peers gossip a fitness score (uptime, capacity, reachability) and route through whoever is best. More nodes → more resilient. You keep one or two always-on anchors as the floor; the rest emerges on top.

5 · Communication — async, blind hub, signed receipts

Store-and-forward, like email — but end-to-end encrypted and the hub is blind. Large files travel as content-addressed encrypted blobs. Delivery and read receipts come back signed by the recipient's Seal — non-repudiable, and the recipient's choice to send. Live voice/chat runs peer-to-peer over WebRTC, hub only signals.

6 · One spine, a widening dial — the five modes

The same spine, hub mesh and fabric run in every mode. As the galaxy widens you rely less on identity, more on earned trust. Family, friends and global are policy on a finished spine — nothing is rebuilt to move along the dial.
